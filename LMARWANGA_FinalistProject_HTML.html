LiteracyIsCreativity

Welcome to Part I of LiteracyIsCreativity, a program of fun, interractive activities that teach literacy skills not through rote exercises and tests, but through creative expression that grows with your skills. Below is an example activity:

We've all heard the Itsy Bitsy Spider poem or sung it in school. The best thing about this poem is the rhymes! A rhyme is made when the sounds at the end of words or at the end of sentences sound the same. Let's read the poem again, and then write our own rhymes for the Itsy Bitsy Spider!

In each stanza, the rhyming words share the same underline.

The Itsy Bitsy Spider

The itsy bitsy spider
Climbed up the waterspout
Down came the rain
And washed the spider out
Out came the sun
And dried up all the rain
And the itsy bitsy spider
Climbed up the spout again
