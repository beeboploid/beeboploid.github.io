I MUST HAVE A PHILOSOPHY
I believe that true motivation lies in fear. The most effective kind of motivation-inducing anxiety springs from the social contracts I form with others and the assurances I give myself that something will be done; on time, on budget. This is a task-managing app that will tap into my primordial fear of dissappointing not only myself but those around me. If this document does not adequately express that intention, I apologize for wasting your time with this proposal.
I MUST MAKE COMMITMENTS

In this app, a 'task' is referred to as a 'commitment.' Each commitment had a deadline, is made 'To' someone, a 'commit-tee', and 'For' a particular task.


MAKING A COMMITMENT:

I MUST KEEP TRACK

As the deadline approaches, commitments expand. The closer the deadline is, the greater speed at which the commitment will expand.

I tap the commitment once in order to remind myself of what is I am meant to do for that person.


SINGLE TAP

I MUST NOT PROCRASTINATE

If the commitment's expansion exceeds a certain threshhold, caused by missing a deadline, the app will send a notification to the commit-tee.


IF I BREAK A COMMITMENT

The commit-tee may respond with a 'prod', notifications aimed at pressuring me into action.


RECEIVING A PROD

I MUST PROVE PROGRESS IS BEING MADE

I tap the commitment twice in order to shrink its size. The app will interpret these taps to mean that I am working on my commitments.


DOUBLE TAP

I MUST AVOID DISAPPOINTMENT

I tap the commitment three times in order to remove it from the list. Three taps indicates that I have fulfilled my commitment to that commit-tee.
